🌌 Deep Space Galaxies

Massive cosmic islands holding billions of stars, planets, and mysteries.

1. The Milky Way

Our home! It is a barred spiral galaxy containing over 100 billion stars. Our entire solar system sits in one of its outer bands, called the Orion Arm, orbiting a supermassive black hole at the very center.

2. Andromeda (M31)

The closest large galaxy to our own, located about 2.5 million light-years away. It is slightly larger than the Milky Way and is racing toward us so fast that our two galaxies will collide and merge in about 4.5 billion years.

3. Sombrero Galaxy (M104)

Named for its striking resemblance to a Mexican hat. It features a massive, incredibly bright central bulb of stars surrounded by a perfectly flat, thick ring of dark cosmic dust lane lanes.

4. Whirlpool Galaxy (M51)

The textbook definition of a perfect spiral galaxy. Its long, elegant arms are loaded with young stars, and its structural gravity is locked in an intense cosmic tug-of-war with a smaller passing companion galaxy.

5. Large Magellanic Cloud

A dwarf satellite galaxy that orbits around the edge of the Milky Way. It has a chaotic, irregular shape due to our massive gravitational pulling force pulling on its stellar structure.

6. Cigar Galaxy (M82)

A famous "starburst" galaxy. It is producing brand-new stars at a speed that is 10 times faster than the Milky Way, triggering violent, glowing red columns of gas blasting out from its center.

7. Black Eye Galaxy (M64)

Features a dark, sinister band of absorbing dust sweeping right across its glowing core. Bizarrely, the gas in its outer sections rotates in the exact opposite direction of the stars in its center!

8. Cartwheel Galaxy

A rare ring-type galaxy. It looked like a normal spiral until a smaller galaxy smashed right through its center like a rock dropped in a pond, sending an enormous ripples of shockwaves and gas outwards.
