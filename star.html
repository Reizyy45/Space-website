✨ Types of Stars

From common yellow dwarfs to massive cosmic giants.

1. The Sun (Yellow Dwarf)

Our home star! It is a main-sequence G-type star that fuses hydrogen into helium, providing the vital light and heat needed to sustain all life on Earth.

2. Sirius (The Dog Star)

The brightest star in our night sky, located in the constellation Canis Major. It is actually a binary system consisting of a bright white star and a faint white dwarf companion.

3. Betelgeuse (Red Supergiant)

A massive, dying red supergiant located in Orion. If placed at the center of our solar system, its massive size would swallow everything up past the orbit of Jupiter.

4. Proxima Centauri (Red Dwarf)

The closest star to our solar system, lying just 4.25 light-years away. It is a dim, low-mass red dwarf star that is completely invisible to the naked eye.

5. Rigel (Blue Supergiant)

A luminous, blazing blue supergiant marking Orion’s left foot. It burns through its fuel incredibly fast and shines roughly 120,000 times brighter than our Sun.

6. Polaris (The North Star)

The anchor of the northern night sky. Because it aligns almost perfectly with Earth's northern rotational axis, it remains fixed in place while all other stars spin around it.

7. Sirius B (White Dwarf)

A stellar remnant left behind after a star dies. It is roughly the physical size of Earth but holds the massive gravitational weight of an entire star, making it intensely dense.

8. Crab Pulsar (Neutron Star)

A highly magnetized, spinning neutron star created in a violent supernova. It rotates exactly 30 times every single second, blinking like a cosmic lighthouse beam.
