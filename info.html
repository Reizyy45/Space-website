🪐 The Eight Planets

Detailed tracking data for our cosmic neighborhood.

🌑 1. Mercury

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It experiences extreme temperature swings, going from brutally hot during the day to freezing cold at night.

🌕 2. Venus

The hottest planet in our solar system due to a thick, runaway greenhouse effect atmosphere. Fun fact: One day on Venus is longer than its whole year!

🌍 3. Earth

Our home planet. It is the only known world in the universe capable of supporting life, complete with vast liquid water oceans and a protective atmosphere.

🔴 4. Mars

The Red Planet. A cold, desert world covered in iron oxide dust, featuring massive dead volcanoes and deep canyons that scientists actively explore with rovers.

🟠 5. Jupiter

The king of the planets and the largest gas giant. It contains a legendary counter-clockwise swirling storm called the Great Red Spot, which is wider than Earth.

🪐 6. Saturn

Famous for its spectacular, expansive ring system made of billions of chunks of ice and rock. It is a light gas giant that could theoretically float in a giant bathtub.

🔷 7. Uranus

An ice giant with a distinct pale blue-green color from atmospheric methane. It is completely unique because it spins sideways, rolling like a bowling ball around the Sun.

🔵 8. Neptune

The most distant planet from the Sun. It is a deep blue, freezing-cold ice giant whipped by the fastest supersonic winds in the entire Solar System.
