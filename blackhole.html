🕳️ Black Holes

Places in space where gravity pulls so hard that even light cannot escape.

1. Sagittarius A* (Sgr A*)

The supermassive black hole sitting directly at the heart of our own Milky Way galaxy. It has a mass equivalent to about 4 million of our Suns compressed into a single point, anchoring everything around it.

2. Messier 87* (M87*)

A historic giant! This supermassive black hole inside the M87 galaxy was the first one ever to be directly photographed by humanity in 2019, showing a glowing, fiery orange ring of superheated plasma gas.

3. Stellar-Mass Black Holes

Formed when a single massive star runs out of fuel and collapses under its own weight in a violent supernova event. They are scattered by the millions throughout our galaxy, usually hiding in total darkness.

4. TON 618 (Ultramassive)

One of the absolute largest monster black holes ever discovered in the entire universe. It weighs a mind-boggling 66 billion times the mass of our Sun, powering a hyper-luminous quasar billions of light-years away.

5. The Event Horizon

Known as the "point of no return." It isn't a solid surface, but a theoretical boundary line surrounding the black hole. Once anything passes this threshold, the required escape velocity exceeds the speed of light.

6. Spaghettification

The extreme gravitational stretching effect. If an astronaut fell feet-first into a black hole, the gravity at their feet would be so much stronger than at their head that they would be stretched out like a piece of spaghetti.

7. Cygnus X-1

Discovered in 1964, this was the very first cosmic object widely accepted to be a black hole. It slowly shreds a massive blue supergiant star next to it, producing intense, glowing X-ray emissions.

8. Intermediate Black Holes

The universe's "missing link." Weighing between 100 to 100,000 stellar masses, they are significantly harder to spot than stellar or supermassive black holes, hiding quietly within compact star clusters.
