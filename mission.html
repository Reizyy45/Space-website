🚀 Space Missions

Humanity's greatest journeys into the great cosmic unknown.

1. Apollo 11 (1969)

The historic NASA mission that successfully landed the first two humans on the Moon. Astronaut Neil Armstrong made history by taking "one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

2. Voyager 1 & 2 (1977)

Launched to tour the outer gas giants, these twin probes sent back stunning close-up data of Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Voyager 1 is now the farthest human-made object from Earth, traveling in interstellar space.

3. Hubble Telescope (1990)

Placed above Earth's blurry atmosphere, Hubble completely revolutionized astronomy. It captured clear, breathtaking images of deep space, helping scientists calculate the exact age of our universe.

4. The ISS (1998)

A brilliant collaboration between multiple nations, creating a permanently inhabited microgravity laboratory in Earth's orbit. Astronauts live and conduct vital scientific research here every single day.

5. Curiosity Rover (2012)

A car-sized robotic rover designed to explore Gale Crater on Mars. Curiosity was built to look for past environmental signs capable of supporting microbial life, discovering ancient liquid water streams.

6. New Horizons (2006)

An interplanetary space probe that conducted a historic flyby of Pluto in 2015. It sent back the world's very first high-resolution pictures of Pluto, revealing its massive, icy heart-shaped glacier plain.

7. James Webb (JWST) (2021)

Humanity's premier space observatory. Using highly advanced infrared vision, JWST peers straight through thick cosmic dust clouds to look at the very first stars and galaxies formed after the Big Bang.

8. Artemis Program (Ongoing)

The current ongoing lunar exploration program aimed at landing the next generation of humans back on the Moon. This mission serves as a stepping stone to build long-term bases and eventually send astronauts to Mars.
