☄️ Comets & Asteroids

The rocky, icy remnants left over from the birth of our Solar System.

1. The Asteroid Belt

A massive, donut-shaped highway of rocks located right between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. It holds billions of asteroids ranging from tiny dust grains to giant dwarf planets.

2. Halley's Comet

The most famous cosmic visitor. It loops around the Sun and swings past Earth once every 75 to 76 years, making it the only predictable comet a human might see twice in their lifetime.

3. Ceres (The Giant)

The absolute king of the Asteroid Belt. Ceres is so exceptionally large that it makes up roughly one-third of the entire belt's mass, earning it a promotion to Dwarf Planet status.

4. Glowing Tails

Comets are essentially "dirty snowballs" of ice and dust. When they fly close to the Sun, the intense heat vaporizes the ice, producing a spectacular glowing trail millions of miles long.

5. The Dino-Killer

Around 66 million years ago, a massive 6-mile-wide asteroid crashed into Earth near Mexico. The violent collision triggered global climate changes that unfortunately brought an end to the dinosaurs.

6. Meteors & Meteorites

A "shooting star" is a meteor burning up safely in our atmosphere. If any piece survives the blazing fall and actually impacts the dirt on the ground, it is renamed a meteorite.

7. 'Oumuamua (The Interstellar Nomad)

Discovered in 2017, this cigar-shaped object was the first-ever confirmed visitor from an entirely different star system. It tumbled past our Sun at incredible speeds before exiting back into deep space.

8. Planetary Defense (DART)

Humanity's shields are up! In 2022, NASA intentionally crashed a spacecraft directly into a small asteroid moon named Dimorphos, successfully proving we can change an asteroid's path to save Earth if needed.
